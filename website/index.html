The Mission

Bridging the Silence Gap

1 in 7 people worldwide experience some form of hearing loss. SignToText makes real-time sign language translation accessible to everyone — no expensive hardware needed.

🤝

The Problem

ASL users face communication barriers in daily life. Interpreters are scarce, expensive, and not always available in the moment communication is needed most.

✨

Our Solution

A browser-based tool that uses your existing webcam and a lightweight CNN model to translate hand signs to text — privately, instantly, and offline-capable.

🧠

The Model

Trained on 87,000 high-quality ASL images across 29 classes (A–Z + del, space, nothing) using a 3-layer CNN with BatchNorm, achieving strong validation accuracy.

What's Next

The Road Ahead

SignToText is just getting started. Here's the roadmap for future versions.

Phase 1 — Now

Single-Letter Recognition

A–Z + del, space, nothing. Live webcam. Flask backend. Browser UI.

Phase 2 — Next

📸 Contribute Training Data

Help the Model Learn Better ASL

Sign in and contribute your own ASL hand-sign captures. Your data is saved separately from the original dataset and used to fine-tune the model — improving accuracy for everyone.

📋

Follow ASL Standards for Quality Contributions

✓ Plain, light or neutral background
✓ Good, even lighting — no harsh shadows
✓ Hand centred in the guide box
✓ Hold the sign steady — no motion blur
✓ Only one hand in frame
✓ Match the ASL reference diagram shown
🗂 Your data is stored in core/user_contributions/ — completely separate from dataset/asl_alphabet_train/ — so it can always be distinguished from the original dataset.
